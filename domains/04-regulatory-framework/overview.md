# Domain 4: Overview of Regulatory Framework

**Weight**: 9% of exam (approximately 7 questions)

---

## Overview

This domain covers the major federal securities laws, regulations, and rules that govern the securities industry, including those established by the SEC and FINRA. While the smallest domain by weight, understanding the regulatory framework is essential.

---

## Key Federal Securities Acts

### Securities Act of 1933 ("Paper Act")
**Focus**: Regulation of NEW issues (primary market)

**Key Provisions**:
- Requires registration of new securities with SEC
- Prospectus must be provided to investors
- Anti-fraud provisions
- Exemptions: Regulation D (private placements), Regulation A

**Liability**: Issuers, underwriters, and others liable for misstatements

---

### Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("People Act")
**Focus**: Regulation of TRADING (secondary market) and industry participants

**Key Provisions**:
- Created the SEC
- Regulates exchanges and broker-dealers
- Requires registration of broker-dealers
- Periodic reporting by public companies (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K)
- Proxy rules
- Insider trading prohibitions (Section 10(b), Rule 10b-5)
- Regulation T (margin requirements)

---

### Investment Company Act of 1940
- Regulates mutual funds and investment companies
- Registration requirements
- 40 Act Fund regulations

---

### Investment Advisers Act of 1940
- Regulates investment advisers
- Fiduciary duty to clients
- Registration with SEC or state

---

### Other Important Acts

**Securities Investor Protection Act of 1970**
- Created SIPC (Securities Investor Protection Corporation)
- Protects customer securities up to $500,000 (including $250,000 cash)
- NOT insurance (protects against broker-dealer failure, not market loss)

**Insider Trading and Securities Fraud Enforcement Act of 1988**
- Increased penalties for insider trading
- Created bounty program for informants

**Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform Act (2010)**
- Response to 2008 financial crisis
- Created Financial Stability Oversight Council (FSOC)
- Volcker Rule

---

## SEC Regulations

**Regulation D**
- Private placement exemption from registration
- Accredited investors

**Regulation A**
- Exemption for small offerings ("mini-IPO")

**Regulation T**
- Federal Reserve Board regulation
- Margin requirements (50% initial margin)

**Regulation S-P**
- Privacy of consumer financial information
- Privacy notices required

**Regulation Best Interest (Reg BI)**
- Broker-dealers must act in customer's best interest
- Enhanced standard of conduct

---

## FINRA Rules

FINRA (Financial Industry Regulatory Authority) is a self-regulatory organization (SRO).

**Key FINRA Rules**:

**Rule 2111 - Suitability**
- Reasonable basis, customer-specific, quantitative suitability
- Replaced by Regulation Best Interest for recommendations

**Rule 2210 - Communications with the Public**
- Retail communications
- Correspondence
- Institutional communications
- Principal approval requirements

**Rule 4512 - Customer Account Information**
- Required account information
- Updates

**Code of Conduct**
- High standards of commercial honor
- Just and equitable principles of trade

**Registration Requirements**
- Securities Industry Essentials (SIE)
- Top-off exams (Series 6, 7, etc.)
- Continuing Education (Regulatory Element, Firm Element)

---

## MSRB (Municipal Securities Rulemaking Board)

- Regulates municipal securities dealers and advisors
- Rules for municipal bond trading
- EMMA (Electronic Municipal Market Access) - disclosure system

---

## Enforcement and Violations

**SEC Enforcement**
- Cease and desist orders
- Civil penalties
- Injunctions
- Disgorgement of profits

**FINRA Enforcement**
- Fines
- Suspensions
- Expulsion
- Letter of Caution

**Criminal Penalties** (Department of Justice)
- Imprisonment
- Criminal fines

---

## Key Concepts

| Act/Regulation | Year | Key Focus |
|---------------|------|-----------|
| Securities Act | 1933 | New issues, registration, prospectus |
| Securities Exchange Act | 1934 | Trading, SEC creation, broker-dealers |
| Investment Company Act | 1940 | Mutual funds |
| Investment Advisers Act | 1940 | Investment advisers |
| SIPC Act | 1970 | Investor protection ($500K coverage) |

**Remember**:
- **1933 Act** = Primary market (new issues)
- **1934 Act** = Secondary market (trading)

This domain tests:

- Distinction between 1933 and 1934 Acts
- SIPC coverage limits and what it covers
- FINRA rules on communications and suitability
