# Domain 1: Knowledge of Capital Markets

**Weight**: 16% of exam (approximately 12 questions)

---

## Overview

This domain covers the fundamental structure of capital markets, how they function, the role of various regulatory agencies, and basic economic factors that influence securities markets. This is foundational knowledge that supports understanding of other domains.

---

## Key Topics

### 1. Market Structure
- **Primary Market vs. Secondary Market**
  - Primary: Where new securities are issued (IPOs, new bond issues)
  - Secondary: Where existing securities are traded (NYSE, NASDAQ)

- **Market Participants**
  - Issuers (corporations, governments)
  - Investors (retail, institutional)
  - Intermediaries (broker-dealers, investment banks)

### 2. Types of Offerings
- **Initial Public Offering (IPO)**
- **Follow-on/Secondary Offerings**
- **Private Placements**
- **Shelf Registrations**

### 3. Regulatory Agencies and Their Functions

| Agency | Full Name | Primary Role |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **SEC** | Securities and Exchange Commission | Federal regulator of securities markets |
| **FINRA** | Financial Industry Regulatory Authority | Self-regulatory organization for broker-dealers |
| **MSRB** | Municipal Securities Rulemaking Board | Regulates municipal securities market |
| **SIPC** | Securities Investor Protection Corporation | Protects customer securities up to $500K |
| **Federal Reserve** | The Fed | Central bank; monetary policy |

### 4. Economic Factors
- **Monetary Policy** (Federal Reserve)
  - Interest rates
  - Money supply
  - Discount rate, Federal Funds rate

- **Fiscal Policy** (Government)
  - Taxation
  - Government spending
  - Budget deficits/surpluses

- **Economic Indicators**
  - GDP (Gross Domestic Product)
  - CPI (Consumer Price Index) - inflation measure
  - Unemployment rate
  - Leading, lagging, and coincident indicators

---

## Key Concepts to Master

1. **Primary vs. Secondary Market** - Understand the difference and examples
2. **Regulatory Roles** - Know which agency regulates what
3. **Monetary vs. Fiscal Policy** - How each affects the economy
4. **Economic Indicators** - What they measure and their significance
