# Domain 2: Understanding Products and Their Risks

**Weight**: 44% of exam (approximately 33 questions) - **HIGHEST WEIGHT**

---

## Overview

This is the most heavily weighted domain on the SIE exam. It covers the characteristics, features, and risks of various securities products including equities, debt instruments, derivatives, and investment companies. Mastering this domain is critical for exam success.

---

## Key Topics

### 1. Equity Securities (Stock)

**Common Stock**
- Ownership interest in a corporation
- Voting rights (typically 1 share = 1 vote)
- Dividends (not guaranteed)
- Capital appreciation potential
- Last in liquidation priority

**Preferred Stock**
- Hybrid security (equity + debt features)
- Fixed dividend (priority over common)
- No voting rights (usually)
- Callable/convertible features
- Higher priority in liquidation than common stock

**Rights & Warrants**
- Rights: Short-term, existing shareholders, trade separately
- Warrants: Long-term, sweetener for bonds, trade separately

**American Depositary Receipts (ADRs)**
- Represents foreign stocks
- Trades in U.S. in dollars
- Currency risk

### 2. Debt Securities (Bonds)

**U.S. Government Securities**
- **T-Bills**: < 1 year, sold at discount, no coupon
- **T-Notes**: 2-10 years, semi-annual interest
- **T-Bonds**: > 10 years, semi-annual interest
- Backed by full faith and credit of U.S. government

**Corporate Bonds**
- Secured vs. Unsecured
- Debentures (unsecured)
- Credit ratings (Moody's, S&P, Fitch)
- Interest rate risk, credit risk

**Agency Securities**
- Issued by government-sponsored enterprises (GSEs)
- Examples: Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, Ginnie Mae
- Mortgage-backed securities (MBS)

### 3. Municipal Bonds

**General Obligation (GO) Bonds**
- Backed by taxing power of the issuer
- Requires voter approval
- Considered safer

**Revenue Bonds**
- Backed by revenue from specific project (toll road, airport)
- No voter approval needed
- Risk tied to project success

**Tax Treatment**
- Interest generally exempt from federal income tax
- May be exempt from state/local tax if issued in investor's state

### 4. Options

**Calls (Right to BUY)**
- Buy call: bullish, limited risk, unlimited profit potential
- Sell call: bearish/neutral, unlimited risk, limited profit

**Puts (Right to SELL)**
- Buy put: bearish, limited risk, substantial profit potential
- Sell put: bullish/neutral, substantial risk, limited profit

**Key Terms**
- Premium: cost of option
- Strike price: exercise price
- Expiration date
- In-the-money, at-the-money, out-of-the-money

### 5. Investment Companies

**Mutual Funds**
- Open-end (continuously issues/redeems shares)
- Priced at NAV (Net Asset Value)
- Redeemed by fund company

**Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)**
- Trades on exchange like stock
- Typically lower fees than mutual funds
- Can be bought/sold throughout the day

**Closed-End Funds**
- Fixed number of shares
- Trades on exchange
- Can trade at premium or discount to NAV

### 6. Other Products

**Variable Annuities**
- Insurance product with investment component
- Tax-deferred growth
- Death benefit

**REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)**
- Invests in real estate
- Must distribute 90% of income as dividends
- Liquidity

**Alternative Investments**
- Private equity
- Hedge funds
- Commodities

## Key Risks to Understand

| Risk Type | Description | Affects Which Products |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Market Risk** | Overall market decline | All securities |
| **Interest Rate Risk** | Bond prices fall when rates rise | Bonds (especially long-term) |
| **Credit/Default Risk** | Issuer fails to pay | Corporate bonds, municipal bonds |
| **Inflation Risk** | Purchasing power erosion | Fixed-income securities |
| **Liquidity Risk** | Difficulty selling quickly | Thinly traded securities |
| **Currency Risk** | Exchange rate fluctuations | ADRs, foreign investments |
| **Reinvestment Risk** | Can't reinvest at same rate | Callable bonds |

## Investment Returns

**Current Yield** = Annual Income / Current Market Price

**Dividend Yield** = Annual Dividend / Stock Price

**Total Return** = Income + Capital Appreciation

**Bond Relationships**:
- Bond prices and interest rates move INVERSELY
- Longer maturity = greater interest rate risk
- Lower credit rating = higher yield (higher risk)
