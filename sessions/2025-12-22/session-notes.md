## Key Concepts Learned

### Concept 1: Preferred Stock vs Common Stock

**Definition/Explanation**:
Preferred Stock (ä¼˜å…ˆè‚¡) differs from Common Stock (æ™®é€šè‚¡) primarily in terms of **priority**, not voting rights.

**Key Points**:
- **Common Stock**:
  - Has voting rights (can participate in company decisions)
  - Receives dividends AFTER preferred shareholders
  - Last in line during liquidation

- **Preferred Stock**:
  - Usually NO voting rights (or very limited)
  - **Priority** in receiving fixed dividends
  - **Priority** in asset distribution during company liquidation
  - Better suited for investors seeking stable income with lower risk

**Real-World Example**:
Imagine you're a retired investor seeking stable income. You would prefer Preferred Stock because:
- You get paid dividends first (priority)
- Fixed dividend amounts (predictability)
- Less concerned about company voting/control
