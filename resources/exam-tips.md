## During the Exam

### Exam Format Reminder

- **75 questions** (all count toward score)
- **1 hour 45 minutes** (105 minutes)
- **Average: 1.4 minutes per question**
- **Multiple choice**: 4 answer choices per question
- **Passing score**: 70% (53 out of 75 questions correct)

---

### Time Management Strategy

**Recommended Approach**:

| Time Checkpoint | Questions Completed | Time Remaining |
|----------------|---------------------|----------------|
| Start | 0 | 105 min |
| 25 minutes | ~18-20 questions | 80 min |
| 50 minutes | ~36-40 questions | 55 min |
| 75 minutes | ~54-58 questions | 30 min |
| 90 minutes | All 75 | 15 min (review) |

**If Running Behind**:
- Don't panic
- Speed up slightly, but stay accurate
- Mark difficult questions and move on
- You have cushion time built in

### Question-Answering Strategies

#### 1. Read Carefully

**Watch for Key Words**:
- **EXCEPT** / **NOT** - looking for the one that doesn't fit
- **ALWAYS** / **NEVER** - usually wrong (too absolute)
- **MOST LIKELY** / **BEST** - choose the most appropriate answer
- **FIRST** / **LAST** - order matters

**Example**:
> "Which of the following is NOT a characteristic of common stock?"

This is asking for the FALSE statement.

---

#### 2. Process of Elimination

**Steps**:
1. Read question completely
2. Eliminate obviously wrong answers
3. Choose between remaining options
4. If stuck between two, trust your first instinct

**Mark Clearly Wrong Answers** (on scratch paper):
- A: âŒ
- B: âŒ
- C: ?
- D: ?

Now choose between C and D.

---

#### 3. Handling Difficult Questions

**If You Don't Know**:
1. Mark it for review (use exam software feature)
2. Make your best guess
3. Move on - don't dwell
4. Come back if time permits

**Never Leave Blank**: There's no penalty for wrong answers, so always guess.

---

#### 4. Common Question Types

**Definition Questions**:
> "What is a debenture?"
- Straightforward - either you know it or you don't
- Use glossary knowledge

**Scenario Questions**:
> "An investor seeks stable income with low risk. Which product is most suitable?"
- Think about product characteristics
- Match to investor profile

**Calculation Questions**:
> "A bond with a 5% coupon and $1,000 par value pays how much interest annually?"
- Use formulas you've memorized
- Double-check math

**Regulatory Questions**:
> "Which act created the SEC?"
- Memorization - know your acts and years

**Comparison Questions**:
> "What is the difference between a GO bond and a revenue bond?"
- Know distinguishing features

### Specific Content Tips

#### Products & Their Risks (44%)

**Most Tested**:
- Equity vs. debt characteristics
- Bond price-yield relationship (inverse)
- Options basics (call vs. put)
- Mutual fund structures (open-end, closed-end, ETF)

**Common Traps**:
- Confusing preferred stock with bonds
- Mixing up GO bonds vs. revenue bonds
- Options: who has unlimited risk (seller of naked call)

---

#### Trading & Accounts (31%)

**Most Tested**:
- Order types (market, limit, stop)
- Account types (JTWROS vs. TIC)
- Prohibited activities (churning, insider trading)
- Settlement dates (T+2)

**Common Traps**:
- JTWROS vs. TIC survivorship rights
- Reg T (50%) vs. maintenance (25%)
- What SIPC covers ($500K including $250K cash) and doesn't cover (market loss)

---

#### Capital Markets (16%)

**Most Tested**:
- Regulatory agencies and their roles
- Primary vs. secondary market
- Basic economic concepts

**Common Traps**:
- Confusing which agency regulates what
- SIPC vs. FDIC (SIPC is NOT insurance)

---

#### Regulatory Framework (9%)

**Most Tested**:
- 1933 Act (new issues) vs. 1934 Act (trading)
- SIPC coverage limits
- FINRA rules

**Common Traps**:
- Mixing up years (1933, 1934, 1940, 1970)
- What SIPC protects against

---

### Mental Strategies

**Stay Calm**:
- If you hit a string of hard questions, don't panic
- Questions are randomized; difficulty varies
- Not everyone gets the same questions

**Trust Your Preparation**:

- Don't second-guess yourself excessively

## Receiving Your Results

**Immediately After Exam**:
- Results displayed on screen: PASS or FAIL
- You'll receive a printed score report

**Pass**:
- Score is valid for 4 years
- You can register for top-off exams (Series 6, 7, etc.)

**Fail**:
- Can retake after 30 days
- Review your diagnostic report (shows weak areas)
- Focus on those topics for retake

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not Reading Questions Fully**
   - Rushing leads to missing key words like "EXCEPT"

2. **Spending Too Long on One Question**
   - If stuck, guess and move on

3. **Changing Answers Without Good Reason**
   - First instinct is usually right

4. **Panicking About Unknown Topics**
   - You don't need 100% - just 70%

5. **Neglecting Prohibited Activities**
   - These are heavily tested and often scenario-based
