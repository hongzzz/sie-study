## Question 1

Which of the following best describes the primary market?

A) The market where existing securities are traded between investors
B) The market where new securities are initially sold to investors
C) The market regulated primarily by FINRA
D) The over-the-counter market for small-cap stocks

**Answer**: B

**Explanation**:
The primary market is where NEW securities are issued and sold for the first time. When a company conducts an IPO or issues new bonds, this occurs in the primary market. The issuer receives the proceeds from these sales.

✗ Why other options are wrong:
- A: This describes the secondary market, where existing securities trade
- C: FINRA regulates broker-dealers in both primary and secondary markets
- D: OTC markets are part of the secondary market

**Key Concepts**: Primary Market, Secondary Market, New Issues
**Difficulty**: ⭐ Easy

## Question 2

Which regulatory agency has the primary responsibility for protecting investors and maintaining fair and orderly securities markets?

A) FINRA
B) MSRB
C) SEC
D) SIPC

**Answer**: C

**Explanation**:
The SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission) is the federal regulatory agency with primary responsibility for protecting investors and maintaining fair, orderly, and efficient markets. It was created by the Securities Exchange Act of 1934.

✗ Why other options are wrong:
- A: FINRA is a self-regulatory organization (SRO) that regulates broker-dealers, but operates under SEC oversight
- B: MSRB regulates the municipal securities market specifically
- D: SIPC protects customer accounts when broker-dealers fail, but is not a regulatory agency

**Key Concepts**: SEC, Regulatory Agencies, Market Regulation
**Difficulty**: ⭐ Easy

## Question 3

An investor purchases shares of stock on the New York Stock Exchange. This transaction occurs in the:

A) Primary market, and the issuer receives the proceeds
B) Primary market, and another investor receives the proceeds
C) Secondary market, and the issuer receives the proceeds
D) Secondary market, and another investor receives the proceeds

**Answer**: D

**Explanation**:
When investors trade existing securities on an exchange like the NYSE, this occurs in the SECONDARY market. The proceeds go to the selling investor, NOT to the issuing company. The company only receives proceeds when it initially issues securities in the primary market.

✗ Why other options are wrong:
- A & B: Exchange trading is secondary market activity, not primary
- C: In secondary market trading, the issuing company does NOT receive proceeds

**Key Concepts**: Secondary Market, Trading, Proceeds
**Difficulty**: ⭐⭐ Medium

## Question 4

Which organization protects customer securities and cash up to $500,000, including $250,000 in cash, when a broker-dealer fails?

A) SEC
B) FDIC
C) SIPC
D) FINRA

**Answer**: C

**Explanation**:
SIPC (Securities Investor Protection Corporation) protects customer accounts when a broker-dealer fails. Coverage is up to $500,000 per customer, including a maximum of $250,000 for cash claims.

Important: SIPC does NOT protect against market losses, only against broker-dealer failure.

✗ Why other options are wrong:
- A: SEC regulates but doesn't provide insurance protection
- B: FDIC insures bank deposits, not securities accounts
- D: FINRA regulates broker-dealers but doesn't provide account protection

**Key Concepts**: SIPC, Investor Protection, Coverage Limits
**Difficulty**: ⭐⭐ Medium

## Question 5

The Federal Reserve's decision to raise interest rates is an example of:

A) Fiscal policy
B) Monetary policy
C) Regulatory policy
D) Market manipulation

**Answer**: B

**Explanation**:
Monetary policy involves the Federal Reserve's control of money supply and interest rates. When the Fed raises or lowers rates, it's using monetary policy to influence the economy.

Key distinction:
- **Monetary Policy**: Federal Reserve → Interest rates, money supply
- **Fiscal Policy**: Government (Congress/President) → Taxation, spending

✗ Why other options are wrong:
- A: Fiscal policy involves government taxation and spending, not Fed interest rate decisions
- C: While the Fed does regulate, changing interest rates specifically is monetary policy
- D: The Fed's rate changes are legitimate policy tools, not market manipulation

**Key Concepts**: Monetary Policy, Federal Reserve, Interest Rates
**Difficulty**: ⭐⭐ Medium

## Question 6

Which of the following economic indicators measures the change in prices of consumer goods and services?

A) GDP (Gross Domestic Product)
B) CPI (Consumer Price Index)
C) Prime Rate
D) Discount Rate

**Answer**: B

**Explanation**:
The CPI (Consumer Price Index) measures inflation by tracking changes in the prices of a basket of consumer goods and services. Rising CPI indicates inflation.

✗ Why other options are wrong:
- A: GDP measures total economic output, not price changes
- C: Prime rate is the interest rate banks charge their best customers
- D: Discount rate is the rate the Fed charges banks for loans

**Key Concepts**: CPI, Inflation, Economic Indicators
**Difficulty**: ⭐ Easy

## Question 7

Which self-regulatory organization (SRO) is responsible for regulating broker-dealers and their registered representatives?

A) MSRB
B) SEC
C) FINRA
D) SIPC

**Answer**: C

**Explanation**:
FINRA (Financial Industry Regulatory Authority) is the SRO that regulates broker-dealers and their employees. FINRA writes rules, conducts examinations, and enforces compliance.

Note: While the SEC oversees FINRA, FINRA is the direct regulator of broker-dealers.

✗ Why other options are wrong:
- A: MSRB regulates municipal securities market participants
- B: SEC is a government agency (not an SRO), though it oversees SROs
- D: SIPC provides account protection, not regulation

**Key Concepts**: FINRA, SRO, Broker-Dealer Regulation
**Difficulty**: ⭐ Easy

## Question 8

All of the following are functions of the Federal Reserve EXCEPT:

A) Setting monetary policy
B) Controlling the money supply
C) Regulating banks
D) Insuring bank deposits

**Answer**: D

**Explanation**:
The Federal Reserve (the "Fed") does NOT insure bank deposits. That's the role of the FDIC (Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation).

The Fed's main functions include:
- Setting monetary policy
- Controlling money supply
- Setting interest rates (discount rate, federal funds rate)
- Regulating and supervising banks

✗ Why other options are correct (we're looking for the EXCEPT):
- A, B, C: All are legitimate Fed functions

**Key Concepts**: Federal Reserve, FDIC, Central Bank Functions
**Difficulty**: ⭐⭐ Medium
**Note**: Watch for "EXCEPT" questions!

## Question 9

A company is conducting its first sale of common stock to the public. This is known as a(n):

A) Secondary offering
B) IPO (Initial Public Offering)
C) Follow-on offering
D) Private placement

**Answer**: B

**Explanation**:
An IPO (Initial Public Offering) is a company's FIRST sale of stock to the public. This marks the company's transition from private to public ownership.

✗ Why other options are wrong:
- A: Secondary offering is when a public company issues additional shares AFTER the IPO
- C: Follow-on offering is another term for secondary offering
- D: Private placement is a sale to a limited number of investors, not to the public

**Key Concepts**: IPO, Primary Market, Public Offerings
**Difficulty**: ⭐ Easy

## Question 10

The MSRB (Municipal Securities Rulemaking Board) primarily regulates:

A) Common stock trading
B) Corporate bond issuance
C) Municipal bond dealers and advisors
D) Mutual fund companies

**Answer**: C

**Explanation**:
The MSRB creates rules for firms that deal in municipal securities (bonds issued by state and local governments) and for municipal advisors.

Important: The MSRB makes the rules, but FINRA and the SEC enforce them for broker-dealers, and the SEC enforces them for municipal advisors.

✗ Why other options are wrong:
- A: Common stock is regulated by SEC and FINRA, not MSRB
- B: Corporate bonds fall under SEC/FINRA jurisdiction
- D: Investment companies are regulated under the Investment Company Act of 1940

**Key Concepts**: MSRB, Municipal Securities, Regulatory Agencies
**Difficulty**: ⭐⭐ Medium
